My commitment to how I will manage my workload in this programme

I am convinced that the number 1 factor that will determine my success in Dev Academy is sleep. When I am well rested, I can manage much larger workloads, focus for longer, am less distracted, more motivated, feel better, think more clearly, problem solve faster, absorb information more deeply, and comprehend a larger portion of what I am learning.

On the flipside, when I a sleep deprived, my motivation, focus, and ability to work plummets. Consequently, the best thing I can do for my learning is to manage my sleep schedule and evenings. I need to prioritise wind-down time and reflections so that I don’t end up watching 3 more episodes of whatever Netflix is on and stay up late. When I do stick to my bedtime and wake time I have noticed that I have about 3 hours of my “prime-time” brain energy first thing in the morning. This is when I can get my best work done and when my mind is at it clearest and highest function. For example, if I wake up at 6:30am, the best thing I can do for the day is immediately get ready and begin coursework at 7pm working straight through until 10am. No doubt, I will need to put in another few hours on top of this each day, and generally as the day goes on my focus and output declines.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

If I become really stuck I have a few ways of attempting to solve the problem. Firstly, is clearly define what it is I need to do or understand. Once I have broken the issue down into a sentence I can then scan my mind for any obvious solutions I may have missed. If a lightbulb moment doesn’t occur at the stage, I can then move on to phrasing my problem in a way that is optimised for “Google.com”. Usually I can find someone with a similar problem who has had it resolved in forums, or there is a clear answer in an educational blog post somewhere. If all else fails, and I am feeling very stuck, I know I can ask my foundations facilitator Lache (who is very friendly and helpful!). I am also aware that one of my close friends is a snr developer and would be happy to sit down and have a chat to me about anything else that comes up.
